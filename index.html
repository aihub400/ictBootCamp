Fewa Lake

Fewa Lake (also spelled Phewa Lake) is a freshwater lake located in Pokhara Valley, in the Kaski District of Gandaki Province, Nepal. It is the second largest lake in Nepal and a popular tourist destination known for its scenic beauty, calm waters, and views of the Annapurna mountain range, including the reflection of Machhapuchhre (Fishtail) Peak.

Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth, with a peak that rises to 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level. It is located in the Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China. Known as "Sagarmatha" in Nepali and "Chomolungma" in Tibetan, Mount Everest is a popular destination for climbers and adventurers from around the world.

Gautam Budhha

Gautam Buddha, also known as Siddhartha Gautama, was the founder of Buddhism and is considered a great spiritual teacher. He was born in Lumbini, Nepal, around the 6th century BCE. After attaining enlightenment under the Bodhi tree, he became known as the Buddha, meaning "The Enlightened One." His teachings focus on peace, compassion, and the path to end suffering through the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path.

Lord shiva

Lord Shiva is one of the principal deities in Hinduism, known as the destroyer and transformer within the Trimurti, which includes Brahma (the creator), Vishnu (the preserver), and Shiva (the destroyer). He is worshipped as the god of meditation, yoga, and cosmic power, and is often depicted with a third eye, a trident (Trishul), and a snake around his neck.

Ilam

Ilam is a hilly district located in Province No. 1 in eastern Nepal, known for its lush green hills, tea gardens, and natural beauty. It is one of the most famous places in Nepal for tea production, often called the "Queen of Hills". Ilam is rich in culture, biodiversity, and scenic landscapes, attracting both domestic and international tourists.

Pasupatinath Mandir

Pashupatinath Temple is one of the most revered and ancient Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, situated on the eastern outskirts of Kathmandu, the capital city of Nepal. It lies along the sacred Bagmati River, which holds deep religious significance for Hindus. The temple is considered one of the holiest shrines in the Hindu religion, especially within Shaivism, the tradition devoted to Lord Shiva.

Pokhara City

Pokhara is one of the most beautiful and important cities in Nepal, located in the central part of the country, within the Gandaki Province. It serves as the capital of this province and is the second-most populous city in Nepal after Kathmandu. Nestled in a serene valley at an altitude of approximately 827 meters (2,713 feet) above sea level, Pokhara is widely recognized for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and role as a major tourism and trekking hub.
